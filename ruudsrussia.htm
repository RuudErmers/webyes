Ruud's Russia CD

Allthough I trade mainly Yes I do have other musical interests. Now I am not going to bother you with other progrock or electronic music, but one thing which is closely related to prog is Russian classical music of the last century. You'll all know the Firebird finale by Strawinsky and probably know one or two pieces by Prokofiev (Romeo and Julliet, The Enemy God).

Well, I am fully into that, in fact, apart from one or two works I have the complete works by Prokofiev (on about 80 CDs!). I've created a disc with some highlights of these, emphasizing on Prokofiev and Russian ballet music.

I sometimes include this disc in my trades, and if you want to have one as well, just let me know, I'll add it for free! But please let me know what you think of it.

This is the content of this CD:

1. Prokofiev

The first part of the cd is devoted to Prokofiev. I've not necessarily included the best tracks, but have tried to go a bit across his works. I want to warn you that these pieces are absolutely not light weighted. You should really play them more than one time. Remember that I am a prog lover like you, so there should be something in it!

1. Scythian Suite (6.29)

The scythian suite is one of the early ballets Prokofiev wrote. It's from about the same date as Stravinsky's "Rite of Spring", which succeeded the Firebird. The theme I have chosen starts very heavily but continues melodicly - this is a trademark of Prokofiev. 


2. On Guard For Peace Opening (8.28)

Talking about heavy stuff! This is it. Prokofiev wrote some very nice choral works, very bombastic with orchestras and choirs. Ivan the terrible is a good example. This work is also very nice and you will notice that in 8 minutes there's a lot that can be done!

3. Violin Concerto. 1 (7.21)

This is an absolutely stunning piece. However it took me years to find this out. Especially the violin is a bit hard to get used to, but if you do, you will discover the real beauty of this piece. If it is too hard for you, you might like to try out the third piano concerto, which is as beautiful as this. Both recommended!


4. Symphony 7, Vivace (8.40)

OK, if you thought the previous tracks were too hard for you try this one. Prokofiev wrote seven symphonies and this is his last one at the peek of his career. Very Russian, yet extremely melodic. What a fine tune this is, it exactly represents Prokofiev stands for.

2. Russian Ballet music

The second part of the cd is devoted to Russian ballet music. It's more light hearted than the first part, yet there's musically very much to be discovered. Ballet music, mostly played at the Bolshoi theater, is one of the cultural exports of Russia and I would love to see the Bolshoi someday. I've acquired a lot of music written for ballets, in the 20th century, and here are some highlights. You will notice that the quality of some pieces isn't perfect, this is correct, as some recordings are quite old (1960 - 1970) and the 10th track even is from LP!

This part starts off with five melodic pieces by Gliere, Glazunov, Schostakowitsch,Khachaturian and Tchaikovsky . It gives you an idea how balletlike this music is. 

After that two absolute highlights in music  are presented. Karayev's "Path of Thunder" is the most stunning Adagio I know. If you don't like this one, there's something wrong with your ears! If anyone gets across a full performance of this ballet let me know (It's about 2 hours). 

Allthough Karayev's piece is my favorite, the following "Humpbacked horse", by Shschedrin is my favorite ballet (yes, I like it more than any of Prokofiev's). You will notice the resemblance with Stravinsky's Firebird, but hey, this ballet lasts for 100 minutes of very fine music. Recommended!

The CD finishes off with the finale of the Firebird. This is a chance for you to listen to some well-known music. It also gives you the chance to think on what the rest is like. You may like the Firebird more than the rest, but I guarantee you, if you give it a chance, the rest is of equal fine quality!
